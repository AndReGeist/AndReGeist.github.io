About

My research focuses on data-driven dynamics simulators. Simulators help us to predict the motion of physical systems such as robots, folding proteins, weather, and energy networks over time. To unlock new applications in these domains, simulators have to be increasingly accurate and reliable. In my research, I look at how we can improve simulator performance by combining physics with machine learning.

I've been a postdoc at RWTH Aachen with Sebastian Trimpe working on physics-informed neuralODEs. During my PhD supervised by Sebastian Trimpe at the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, I developed probabilistic models to learn implicitly-constrained robot dynamics. In collaboration with Jonathan Fiene, we developed the "Wheelbot" being a robot platform for teaching control theory and robotics.

During my Master's in Theoretical Mechanical Engineering at TU Hamburg, I worked on end-to-end vehicle control as a research intern at the Vehicle Dynamics and Control laboratory of the University of California, Berkeley and as a research student on autonomous field exploration via AUV swarms working with Eugen Solowjow.
